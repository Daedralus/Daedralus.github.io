Tournament Rules

Most rules are continued from the 2nd Annual WoL Jamboree. However, as this is a different format tournament, be sure to read through the rules for any changes that may have occured.

Version Restrictions

This tournament will be played with the Wars of Liberty mod, so all players must have the latest version of Wars of Liberty installed. The current version is "Nordic Patch", released 18th of October 2019. If you downloaded the mod before this date, please run your updater to ensure that you have the correct version. If you do not have the mod, you can download it here. Simply download and run the installer, following the onscreen instructions, or watch this YouTube video for help.

Civilization Restrictions

In a Best-of-3, you must win with 2 different civilizations.
In a Best-of-5, you must win with 3 different civilizations.
In a Best-of-7, you must win with 4 different civilizations.

For the 1st game in a series, players will agree on a civilization matchup. After that, the winner of the previous game will select their civilization first. This pick is final and will be locked in. Their opponent will then pick their civilization afterwards.

Once you have won with a civilization, you may not use it again for the rest of the series. Losing a game does not disable a player’s civilization from being played again. In addition, once a player has won with a civilization from the base AoE3 game, none of the other base civilizations may be played. For example, winning with China then precludes a player from playing with Britain, Iroquois, Ottoman, etc.

Note: The tournament staff may ban a civilization from play if deemed necessary. Previous games using that civilization will not be penalized.


Gameplay Rules

1.1 - All games must be played on the Wars of Liberty mod.

1.2 - Every game must be recorded.

1.3 - Winners must submit recorded games using the Match Results Submitter found here.

1.4 - Every player must respect their opponent, casters and organizers. Misbehavior** will result in a warning and can lead to disqualification. Racism and/or insults will not be tolerated. More information regarding this can be found in the Disqualification Rules section.

1.5 - When a player disconnects from the internet BEFORE 2 minutes, the player can (when both agree) restart and replay the game. When the opponent does not agree to restart, the outcome of the game will be determined by the tournament staff.

1.6 - When a player disconnects AFTER 2 minutes, the players can (when both agree) restart and replay the game. When the opponent does not agree to restart, the player who lost connection will be given a loss.

1.7 - Every player may restart a game BEFORE the 2 minute mark when necessary. This is allowed ONCE per map when the game is NOT casted by an official caster. Restarts for CASTED games will be determined by the caster him/herself.

1.8 - The caster is allowed to restart a game as many times as necessary for optimal play.

1.10 - Recorded games must be submitted with match results. Failure to do so may result in a match being considered invalid.

1.11 - Players must be respectful to each other when selecting civilizations during the first game in a series. Players cannot intentionally change to unfair match-ups, or otherwise be difficult to agree with. The tournament staff reserves the right to two possible outcomes. 1) If only one player is deemed challenging, then that player will be forced to pick their civilization first. 2) If both players are disagreeable, then a blind pick will be enforced.


Skill-Group Format & Seeding Rules

2.1 - In addition to the civilization restrictions, base game civilizations (e.g. Ottomans, French etc) cannot be played in Best-of-3 or below in a Skill-Group Tournament.

2.2 - For proper seeding, players must sign up with their highest rated account. After seeding, and upon special request, a player's sign-ups account may be swapped with their most "known" account.

2.3 - Seeding for this tournament is simpler as the rank of the player is considered as the major component. No additional adjustments will be made towards elo on top of current rank.

2.4 - As mentioned in 2.2, the highest rating throughout all accounts is considered and players can only sign-up for that skill-group. Example: If you are currently PR14, but your highest is PR20, then you will only be eligible for the 2nd Lieutenant Group. On certain cases, soft adjustments may be allowed.

2.5 - Tournament administrators reserve the right to manually adjust the seeding.

2.6 - From Conscript rank until Captain (with Captain excluded), players will have their own divisions. However, Captains and Majors will be combined in their own group, and all ranks above those will be in their separate group. See table below.


Disqualification Rules

3.1 - All external exploits and game modification software are forbidden. The tournament administration reserves the right to disqualify a player for use of any exploits to gain an unfair advantage.

3.2 - Exploits include but are not limited to: Map Hacks, Trainers, Explorer Revival Glitch, Explorer ROF Glitch, OOS / Intentional Lag (Stacked downloads or other game crashing exploits), UIs (Ekanta, Transparent UI), and foreign mods (zoom modification).

3.3 - If you suspect your opponent has violated any of the player integrity rules, finish the game and submit it for review with an explanation of why you think the game was unfair. If the tournament staff members find someone to be guilty of cheating, the cheater will be disqualified from the tournament.

3.4 - The tournament staff has the right to disqualify a player in cases where there is severe lag to the point where the playing and/or viewing experience is unusually burdened.

3.5 - The registered account name must be the highest rated account of its owner for proper seeding.

3.6 - One-Touch Forfeiture: Once a player has expressed that he has forfeited the match, he will be disqualified from the tournament and not permitted to continue playing. Having an insufficient reason for forfeiting may result in a tournament ban.

3.7 - "Smurfing", playing for another user, or having another user play your match for you, is subject to immediate disqualification for both parties involved.

**Misbehaviour includes excessive shit-talking and disrespecting casters and staff. More severe misbehaviour such as racism will not be tolerated in any form and may result in immediate disqualification from the tournament.


Scheduling Rules

4.1 - To keep this website simple and as an informal area, scheduling will be done through Daedralus's Discord Server. Players will then be assigned roles that match their skill-group and be allocated to their own special channels in the discord server.

4.2 - Players must respond with the times they are available to play in their dedicated channel. Players will then play out their games on the given maps.

4.3 - Posts in server channels will be considered along with interaction with admins when making decisions regarding scheduling. Other communications will not be considered.

4.4 - Players may issue their vetoes any time before the series.

4.5 - If a player forgets to issue his veto before a series begins, the last map in the pool will automatically be removed.

4.6 - Players must play the series in the map order given, after vetoes have been issued.

4.7 - In the case a certain map continously gives errors and becomes unplayable, the next map from the map-pool can be played. Otherwise, a map from the "Backup-Pool" can be selected. See "Map-Pool" for more information.
